Research Overview

This ongoing research project examines Anton Chekhov's profound influence on 20th-century Eastern and Western literatures through comprehensive comparative analysis. By studying Chekhov's original Russian works alongside their Vietnamese adaptations, I explore how cultural contexts shape narrative interpretation and literary translation.

The research bridges cultural understanding with literary analysis, examining how Chekhovian realism and social critique transcend linguistic and cultural boundaries while adapting to local contexts.

Research Questions

  • How do Chekhov's narrative techniques and character psychology translate across cultural contexts?
  • What elements of Chekhovian realism resonate universally versus those requiring cultural adaptation?
  • How do Vietnamese adaptations preserve or transform Chekhov's social critique?
  • What role does translation play in shaping literary influence across cultures?
  • How can comparative literature inform cross-cultural UX design principles?

Points of Convergence

  • Commitment to portraying human vulnerability with empathy rather than judgment

  • Multi-perspective narrative techniques that require active reader interpretation
